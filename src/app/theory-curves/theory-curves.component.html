Theory: Curves

In this theory chapter we will briefly talk about the theory of curves to get a basic understanding on what they are, how they could be created in Blender in specific and what they can be used for. There are two types of curves available in Blender that can also be found in any other 3D modelling tool, namely Bezier Curves and Nurbs which are both heavily used for CGI and CAD applications.

Bezier Curves

The image above displays a very simple Bezier Curve. As you can see the curve consists of two endpoints. In addition there can be multiple control points between them which are used to determine the overall shape of the curve. For each of those points there are handles that are used for editing the curve. I will not go into the mathematical details here. In case you are interested please take a look here.

NURBS(Non-Uniform Rational B-Splines)

On the first sight they look pretty similar. In fact NURBS curves can be seen as a generalisation of Bezier curves. They are especially good for the creation of organic models since they can be used for surfaces. The shape of these curves is determined by multiple control vertices to which the curve is pulled. By for example extruding one curve along another, surfaces can be created which then at render time will be converted into polygons. Their points are therefore determined by the weighted sum of control points.
